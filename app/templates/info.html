Introduction

Welcome to CyberToolkit! This page provides information about our system, including details about Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs), how we calculate vulnerability scores, and more.

About CVEs

Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs) are unique identifiers assigned to known cybersecurity vulnerabilities. Each CVE entry contains information about a specific vulnerability, including a description, affected software, and a severity score.

Understanding CVE Scores

CVE scores, also known as CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System) scores, provide a numerical representation of the severity of a vulnerability. Scores range from 0.0 to 10.0, with higher scores indicating greater severity.

The severity of a CVE score is typically categorized as follows:

  • 0.0 - 3.9: Low severity
  • 4.0 - 6.9: Medium severity
  • 7.0 - 8.9: High severity
  • 9.0 - 10.0: Critical severity

Weighting Average Scores

At CyberToolkit, we calculate a weighted average vulnerability score based on the severity of CVEs present in a system. Each CVE score is assigned a weight based on its severity level, and these weights are used to calculate the overall vulnerability score.
